# Privacy Policy — ruta

_Last updated: 2026-06-22_

ruta is a social browser for Android. This policy explains what data the app does and does not
handle.

## What ruta collects

**Nothing.** ruta has no accounts, no analytics, no advertising, no crash reporting, and no
third-party tracking SDKs. The developer does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal
information. There are no servers operated by ruta that receive your data.

## Data that stays on your device

All app data — your settings, bookmarks, open tabs, profiles, and the cookies/logins for the
sites you visit — is stored **only on your device**. It is excluded from cloud backup. Uninstalling
the app removes it.

## Network connections ruta makes

To function, ruta makes a small number of network requests on your behalf:

- **The websites you open.** When you open a site (X, Instagram, Mastodon, etc.) ruta loads it in a
  WebView, exactly like any browser. Those sites receive your activity and apply their own privacy
  policies. ruta blocks their ads and trackers but does not make them anonymous.
- **Ad-block filter lists.** ruta downloads public filter lists (EasyList, EasyPrivacy, and any
  lists you add) from their servers to keep blocking up to date.
- **Site icons.** For custom sites and tabs, ruta fetches a small favicon from DuckDuckGo's icon
  service (`icons.duckduckgo.com`), which receives the site's domain in order to return its icon.

None of these requests include personal identifiers added by ruta.

## Permissions

- **Internet / Network state** — to load websites and filter lists.
- **Camera / Microphone** — only used if a website you visit requests them (e.g. to upload a photo
  or record audio) and you grant permission. ruta never accesses them on its own.
- **Notifications / Storage** — used for downloads you initiate.

## Children

ruta is a general-purpose browser and is not directed at children.
